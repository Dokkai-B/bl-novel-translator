Jiang Tian  

"The sound of cicadas that summer was more annoying than any year before. The branches outside the classroom window grew wildly, yet they could never block out the blazing sun." The big break on the top floor of Mingli Building at the affiliated school was always noisy. The class monitor of Class A, Grade Two drifted into the classroom from the corridor, shouting, "Attention—our class has a new student coming in!"  
"Another trick from the little eunuch in the administration office," someone teased.  
"You’re the little eunuch! I’m being serious," came the retort.  
"It’s neither midterm nor finals, who’s coming in?"  
"A transfer student."  
Upon hearing this, everyone in the classroom who was awake perked up: "Is it a boy or a girl? Is it for real?"  
"Absolutely true! I just saw him—a boy, fair-skinned, quite handsome." The class monitor smacked his lips and added, "I wonder which teacher doesn’t want to be a decent human being and brought someone else’s school heartthrob over."  
Chaos erupted in the classroom as the girls took the opportunity to sneak glances at the corner of the last row. There, a boy lay on the desk catching some sleep, one hand covering the back of his head, his long fingers slightly bent, and prominent wrist bones visible.  

It was too noisy around him. He scratched his short hair, turned his head in another direction.  

The girls withdrew their gazes, and the noise immediately softened: "Where did he transfer from?"  
The class monitor reported a school name.  
"What the heck? Is there even such a school around?"  
"I've never heard of it, but it must be a provincial key school, or he wouldn't be able to transfer to our class."  
"Wait a minute, let me check." The boy who spoke stealthily dug his phone out from under the table: "No teachers coming, right? Keep an eye out for me."  
His fingers moved swiftly as he searched, and after finishing, he was left stunned: "What the hell?"  
"What’s wrong?"  
The boy held up his phone and showed the screen in a circle, leaving everyone in shock.  
After a while, someone finally snapped back: "He’s from another province? Transferring here after finishing the first year of high school, to take the college entrance exam? Is that handsome guy out of his mind?"  
The one perceived to be out of his mind, Sheng Wang, was waiting for an arrangement in the academic office.  
The cicadas were loudly chirping amidst the dense shade as he moved away from the window and put on his headphones, finally able to hear the new voice messages sent by his father. Three consecutive messages, each lasting a minute, typical of Sheng Mingyang's style.  
"Your Uncle Chen just called and said you went upstairs by yourself. Why didn’t you wait for him? It’s a new place with new classmates; it’s better to have someone with you—"  
"How’s the school environment? Is it very different compared to Yi Zhong? Though both are provincial key schools, they’re not in the same province—"  
"Have you seen Old Xu—"  
The air conditioning in the political education office was a bit outdated and only provided localized cooling, suitable for middle-aged friends. Sheng Wang stood under the airflow, the slight moisture on the ends of his hair blowing cool. He tapped the screen, listening to only the gist of each voice message before cutting them off, rolling his eyes at the third one, feeling a bit bewildered.  
Of course, he knew who Uncle Chen was—the driver who brought him to report. Cars weren’t allowed in the teaching area, and the parking lot was quite far. Sheng Wang didn’t want to exert himself, so he simply sent him back.  
So…  
"Who's Old Xu?" Sheng Wang pressed the send button.  
"You interrupted my voice message again?" Sheng Mingyang replied immediately.  
Sheng Wang fanned himself by tugging at his collar, pretending he had lost the connection.  
Sheng Mingyang called back, sounding exasperated: "Old Xu is the director of the political education office, not very tall, looks pretty upright, might be a bit serious. He should have welcomed you, did you meet him?"  
Following his description, Sheng Wang tried to recall: "I don’t think so. The teacher who came to bring me upstairs was quite amiable, always smiling, just looked like a big-mouthed monkey."  
He was also short, barely reaching Sheng Wang's shoulder, needing to look up to speak to him. He left Sheng Wang there and went downstairs, saying he’d find someone to get the new textbooks.  
Sheng Mingyang paused a moment: "Oh, that must be him."  
Sheng Wang: "…"  
He thought for a moment and asked, "Dad, do you think I look upright?"  
Sheng Mingyang wanted to hit him.  
As a businessman, he was proficient at speaking appropriately to anyone, but he couldn't maintain his composure with his son.  
Voices came from outside, and Sheng Wang glanced over: "Monkey—no, Director Xu is here, I’ll hang up."  
Sheng Mingyang increased his tempo: "Okay, good performance. Make sure to leave a good impression on the teacher on the first day, don’t give out meaningless nicknames."  
"Okay," Sheng Wang replied lazily.  
"Tonight let Uncle Chen pick you up. I should be back home around that time too; take you to—"  
He hesitated for a moment, then pretended to be relaxed and natural: "We’ll treat Aunt Jiang to dinner, the same thing we discussed last time, alright?"  
Sheng Wang pressed his lips together.  
Aunt Jiang's name was Jiang Ou, and she had a son. He had never seen Jiang Ou in person, only looked at two photos, which was rather perfunctory.  
This name had been on his ears for nearly a year, its frequency increased from once every couple of months to nearly daily; he was about to get used to it. It must be said that Sheng Mingyang was a master at controlling the pace, leaving no room for error. Even when Sheng Wang wanted to throw a tantrum, he could hardly find a proper angle to approach.  
Last month, Sheng Mingyang said he would be doubly busy in the second half of the year, and wouldn’t be home for many days. He also mentioned that there had been some issues with Jiang Ou, and she couldn’t live in her house anymore. So, he wanted her to move in, giving her a place to stay while also helping to look after Sheng Wang.  
In reality, the looking after part was a facade; there were dedicated maids for cleaning and cooking. The so-called issues might not even be true, simply a pretext, because once they lived together, could she really leave?  
This matter was labeled as a discussion, but by the time Sheng Wang had time to agree, new items had already begun appearing at home, all preparing to welcome that woman, oh, and her son as well.  
Whether to have dinner tonight was only destined for one outcome.  
Delays in getting a response from Sheng Wang made Sheng Mingyang call out to him from the other end.  
At that moment, the big-mouthed monkey, Director Xu, happened to walk in, and Sheng Wang paused momentarily and hung up the phone.  
After all, it was new student registration, and Director Xu could still maintain a basic friendliness: "Were you on the phone with your family? No problem, no need to rush off, just let us know."  
Sheng Wang turned around, smiling with youthful exuberance: "Thank you, teacher. I was just finishing up."  
Director Xu nodded to the teacher behind him while pointing at Sheng Wang. He had just mentioned downstairs that this transfer student, although he had the face to charm young girls, appeared to be a good student who wouldn’t act out.  
"Come, have a seat." Director Xu pointed to a small stack of books in the new area: "This is the textbook you’re theoretically supposed to use this semester; you can take a look."  
What does theoretically mean?  
Sheng Wang didn’t quite understand the meaning of that. He flipped through the top chemistry book for a couple of pages, finding the content quite consistent with what he had studied before; there shouldn’t be any issues with learning it.  
"I looked at your previous records; you’ve transferred schools several times?" Director Xu said.  
Sheng Wang nodded slightly: "Yes, I’ve transferred a few times." Basically, he followed Sheng Mingyang around.  
He attended elementary school in Jiangsu, transferred twice from the first year to the first year of high school; this was the third time. Thanks to these experiences, he didn’t have much attachment to any place and couldn’t stay long anywhere.  
"I’ve also reviewed the transcripts; you’re an outstanding student, consistently ranking among the top three in your grade during exams. Your aptitude is definitely sufficient. However, the arrangement and progress of courses between both schools might have small differences," Director Xu comforted, with his fingers measuring a distance of less than 1 centimeter. "It’s common to encounter such problems when transferring schools, just put in a little effort, and you can catch up, don’t be afraid."  
Sheng Wang, who had always sailed through his studies, had never truly been afraid of anyone, so it was unlikely for him to be scared. However, he couldn’t appear too proud; he could only lower his head: "I was prepared before coming here; I’ll work hard to keep up."  
Director Xu smiled kindly: "Did you have any pre-scheduled subjects in the first year?"  
Sheng Wang replied: "No, the school tried a streaming system for a semester."  
"Oh," Director Xu nodded, "actually, ours is also a streaming system, just a bit different."  
Sheng Wang was a bit confused: "Different? How's it different?"  
"The class A you are soon to join is the Grade Two enhanced chemistry and physics class; we switch classes halfway through the semester. You know, there are midterms and finals, each time the last three in those exams are moved down to class B, and then we bring in the top three from class B to replace them. That’s the kind of streaming we have."  
Sheng Wang: "……"  
In short, theirs was a method of selection for classes; theirs was a method of kicking out the failures.  
Director Xu had scared the little kid enough; he finally decided to act human.  
He led Sheng Wang through the flower corridor toward Mingli Building. Passing by a wall of honors, Sheng Wang couldn’t help but glance a few more times, as that wall of expressionless "ID photos" looked too much like a wanted poster.  
The school’s aesthetics was incredible, he thought.  
However, Director Xu raised on his toes, dramatically displaying a peacock spreading its feathers, proudly saying: "The number of competitions in the first year isn't too many, but our performance has been quite good. Most of the people on this wall will become your classmates; you can get to know them in advance."  
Sheng Wang, being face-blind, had no interest in recognizing classmates ahead of time; he remembered only one.  
First, this one had a high repetition rate; with his own strength, he turned the honor wall into a matching game; second, his surname was Jiang, named Jiang Tian.  
The emphasis was on the second.  
Sheng Wang admitted that if he were an emperor, he would definitely be a tyrant, keen on collective punishment. He might as well be petty, as he recently found himself not liking anyone with the surname Jiang.  
For the thousandth time, Director Xu admired the wall, but suddenly his expression darkened. He approached the photo of Jiang Tian, wiped it a couple of times, and angrily exclaimed: "Who messed up the honor wall with heart doodles? This is so irregular!"  
Sheng Wang added, "There’s more than one person who drew."  
The school photographer had quite a unique style, but the people in those photos still retained some sort of characteristic; in Director Xu's words, they could easily charm young girls. However, Sheng Wang thought these cold-toned boys would likely be the bad guys.  
He prayed that in the future, he could stay away from this one, lest they end up clashing one day.  
However, not five minutes after wishing that, he found himself pressed next to the real-life villain. The reason was that at the beginning of tracking the progress, it would be a bit challenging, and the best advice was to seek help from his deskmate.  
Director Xu said: "Looking across the entire grade, I doubt you could find a more suitable deskmate than Jiang Tian."  
As soon as the words fell, forty pairs of eyes in the classroom directed suffocating gazes toward him.  
Sheng Wang glanced awkwardly at the big-mouthed monkey, thinking, "What the hell."
