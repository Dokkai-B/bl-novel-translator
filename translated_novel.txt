Jiang Tian

"The cicadas in that summer were louder than any other year; the branches outside the classroom windows grew wildly, yet they could not block the harsh sunlight." The large break on the top floor of Mingli Building at the affiliated secondary school was always noisy. The class representative of Class 2A drifted into the classroom from the corridor, shouting, "Report—our class has a new student!"  
"Another scam from the little eunuchs in the office." Someone teased.  
"Who are you calling a little eunuch? I'm serious!"  
"It's neither mid-term nor finals; what new student?"  
"A transfer student."  
Upon hearing this, everyone awake in the classroom perked up: "Is it a boy or a girl? Is it for real?"  
"Absolutely! I just saw him, a boy, quite fair and handsome." The representative added after a moment, "I don’t know which teacher isn’t doing their job and brought the campus heartthrob here."  
An uproar erupted in the classroom, and a few girls, taking advantage of the chaos, peeked at the corner of the last row. There lay a boy sprawled on the desk, catching up on sleep, one hand covering the back of his head, long fingers slightly curled, and prominent wrist bones.  
It was too noisy around him; he scratched his short hair and turned his head in the opposite direction.  
The girls retracted their gazes, their voices immediately dropping: "Where did he transfer from?"  
The representative mentioned a school name.  
"What’s that? There’s a school around here?”  
"I haven't heard of it either, but it must be a provincial key school; otherwise, he wouldn't have transferred to our class."  
"Wait, let me check." The speaking boy stealthily pulled out his phone from under the desk: "Is there no teacher coming? Keep an eye out for me."  
His fingers moved swiftly; after a quick search, he was dumbfounded: "What the hell?"  
"What happened?"  
The boy displayed his phone, and everyone else was equally astonished.  
After a moment, someone finally snapped back: "He’s from another province? Transferred here after finishing the first year? To take the college entrance exam? Is that handsome guy nuts?"  
The one who was losing his mind, Sheng Wang, was currently waiting for arrangements in the Academic Affairs Office. The cicadas cried out in the thick shade; he left the window and put on headphones to better hear the voice message his dad had just sent. Three messages in a row, each a minute long, consistent with Sheng Mingyang’s style.  
"Uncle Chen just called me and said you went upstairs by yourself. Why didn’t you wait for him? New place, new classmates; it's better to have someone guiding you..."  
"How’s the school atmosphere? Is it very different from the previous No. 1 Secondary School? Although they’re both provincial key schools, after all, they’re not in the same province..."  
"Have you seen Old Xu yet..."  
The air conditioning in the political and educational office was a bit old, only providing partial cooling, suitable for older folks. Sheng Wang stood by the vent, the slight dampness in the ends of his hair was blown cold. He tapped the screen with his finger, cutting off each voice message after the summary, rolling his eyes at each one until he was a bit dazed by the third.  
He knew Uncle Chen was the driver who brought him to report. The teaching area didn't allow cars inside, and the parking lot was far away. Sheng Wang was tired of walking, so he simply let Uncle Chen leave first.  
So…  
"Who is Old Xu?" Sheng Wang pressed the send button.  
"You cut me off again?" Sheng Mingyang replied immediately.  
Sheng Wang fanned himself by tugging at his collar, pretending the network was down.  
Sheng Mingyang called back, sounding quite helpless: "Old Xu is the head of the political and educational office; he’s not too tall but has a proper appearance, might be a bit stern. He was supposed to meet you; did you see him?"  
Sheng Wang recalled following his description: "I don’t think so. The teacher who brought me upstairs was quite amiable, smiling the whole time, just looked like a big-mouthed monkey."  
He was also short, only just barely reaching Sheng Wang’s shoulder, needing to look up to speak. After settling Sheng Wang in, he went downstairs to fetch new textbooks.  
Sheng Mingyang paused: "Oh, that’s probably him."  
Sheng Wang: "…"  
He thought for a moment and asked, "Dad, do you think I have a decent appearance?"  
Sheng Mingyang wanted to hit him.  
As a businessman, he was fluent in talking with people, whether human or ghost, but couldn't keep his composure with his son.  
Voices came from outside, and Sheng Wang leaned over to take a look: "Monkey—no, Director Xu is here; I need to hang up."  
Sheng Mingyang quickened his speech: "Okay, perform well, try to leave a good impression on the teacher on the first day, and don't give silly nicknames."  
"Okay," Sheng Wang replied lazily.  
"Uncle Chen will pick you up in the evening, and I should be home by then too. We can..."  
He hesitated for a moment, then forced a relaxed tone: "We’ll take Aunt Jiang out for dinner, just like we discussed last time, okay?"  
Sheng Wang pressed his lips together.  
Aunt Jiang, named Jiang Ou, had a son. He had never met Jiang Ou in person; he had only seen two photos, and even those had been pretty much a blur.  
He had been hearing that name intermittently for almost a year, the frequency increasing from every couple of months to nearly daily, and he was starting to get used to it. It must be said that Sheng Mingyang was a master at controlling the rhythm, leaving no room for mistakes. So much so that even when Sheng Wang wanted to throw a fit, he couldn't find a proper angle.  
Last month, Sheng Mingyang said he would be twice as busy in the second half of the year, hardly staying at home, and mentioned some incidents on Jiang Ou's side that made her house uninhabitable. Therefore, he wanted Jiang Ou to move over, so she would have a place to stay and could also help look after Sheng Wang.  
Actually, the care was a pretense; cleaning and cooking were handled by a dedicated auntie. The incident wasn’t necessarily true, maybe just an excuse to have a breakthrough; after all, once they lived together, could she still leave?  
This matter was termed a discussion, but in reality, before Sheng Wang nodded, new items had already begun appearing at home. Everything was being prepared for the arrival of that woman—oh, and her son.  
Regardless of whether they had dinner tonight, the outcome was already certain.  
After a long silence with no response from Sheng Wang, Sheng Mingyang called out to him again.  
Just then, the big-mouthed monkey-like Director Xu walked in, and Sheng Wang paused for a moment before hanging up.  
After all, it was a new student report day. Director Xu still managed to maintain a basic warmth: "On the phone with home? That's fine, no need to rush to hang up; it’s expected."  
Sheng Wang turned around, smiling with youthful energy: "Thank you, Teacher; I was just about done talking."  
Director Xu nodded to the teachers behind him while pointing at Sheng Wang. He had just mentioned downstairs that although this new transfer student had a face that could easily attract young girls, he was clearly a well-behaved student who wouldn’t cause trouble.  
"Come, sit," Director Xu said, pointing to a small stack of books that had just arrived: "These are the textbooks you'll be using this semester; you can take a look."  
What does "theoretically" mean?  
Sheng Wang was momentarily confused by this wording. He flipped through the top chemistry book for a couple of pages. The content connected well with what he had learned before, it was mostly similar, and there shouldn't be too many issues studying it.  
"I’ve seen your previous records; you’ve transferred several times?" Director Xu asked.  
Sheng Wang nodded: "Yes, I’ve transferred a few times." It was mostly following Sheng Mingyang around.  
He studied in Jiangsu during elementary school, then transferred twice between junior high and high school, making this the third time. Thanks to these experiences, he felt no attachment to anywhere, and he never stayed for long.  
"I’ve also looked at your report card; you’re an excellent student, rarely falling out of the top three in grades. You clearly have the aptitude, but there might be slight differences in course arrangement and pace between the two schools," Director Xu comforted, gesturing about a centimeter with his hand, "It’s normal to encounter such issues when transferring; as long as you pay a little attention, you can catch up. Don’t be afraid."  
Sheng Wang had an easy time with his studies and had never really felt daunted by anyone; he definitely wasn't worried about that. But he couldn't appear too arrogant; he simply lowered his tail: "I prepared mentally before coming, and I will try to keep up."  
Director Xu softened further: "Did you have pre-major subjects in your first year?"  
Sheng Wang replied: "No, the school tried a rotating class system for a semester."  
"Oh," Director Xu nodded, "Actually, we also have a rotating class system, just a bit special."  
Sheng Wang was puzzled: "Special? How is it special?"  
"The class you’re about to join, Class A, is a science and chemistry enhancement class; we rotate every half semester. There are two big exams, mid-term and final; after each exam, the bottom three are demoted to Class B, and we invite the top three to join in. This is how our rotation works."  
Sheng Wang: "…"  
In short, theirs was a method of selecting courses, while theirs was a method of elimination.  
After enough frightening the little guy, Director Xu finally decided to act more like a person.  
He led Sheng Wang through the flower corridor toward the Mingli Building. As they passed an honor wall, Sheng Wang couldn’t help but glance a few more times, because that wall of expressionless "ID photos" looked too much like wanted posters.  
The school had a unique sense of aesthetics, he thought.  
Director Xu, however, tiptoed and performed a peacock display in place, proudly saying: "The number of competitions in the first year isn’t that many, but we performed quite well. Most of the people on this wall will become your classmates, so you can familiarize yourself in advance."  
Sheng Wang was face-blind and had no interest in familiarizing himself with his classmates. He only remembered one of them.  
Firstly, this one had a notably high rate of repetition, turning the honor wall into a matching game; secondly, he had the surname Jiang, named Jiang Tian.  
The emphasis was on the second point.  
Sheng Wang believed that if he were an emperor, he would surely be a tyrant who loved to punish those associated with him. Though petty, he could not help but feel uneasy around anyone with the surname Jiang lately.  
After observing the honor wall for the thousandth time, Director Xu suddenly pulled a long face. He stepped closer to Jiang Tian's photo and angrily wiped it a couple of times, saying: "Who drew love hearts on the honor wall? No decorum!"  
Sheng Wang added fuel to the fire from the side: "It's not just one person who drew."  
The school photographer was indiscriminate, but the people in the photos still possessed a certain trait; in Director Xu's words, they could charm young girls. However, Sheng Wang felt that this kind of cool-toned boy was most likely part of the Bking crew.  
He prayed that in the future, he could keep his distance from this guy so as not to get into a fight someday out of sheer irritation.  
As fate would have it, within five minutes of making that wish, he was pressed next to the real-life version of Bking by Director Xu. The reason given was that initially catching up with the pace might be a bit challenging, and the best method was to seek help from a desk partner.  
Director Xu said: "In the entire grade, it's likely you won't find a more suitable desk partner than Jiang Tian."  
As soon as the words left his mouth, more than forty pairs of eyes in the class turned to them with shocked expressions.  
Sheng Wang cast a dry glance at the big-mouthed monkey, thinking to himself, "You must be kidding me."
