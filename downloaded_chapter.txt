“Teacher, someone is looking for you.” A girl called out to Director Xu, pointing out the window. The hand that had been resting on Sheng Wang's shoulder finally withdrew. Director Xu nodded to the person outside who was looking for him and said, “Going to a meeting, right? I'll be right there.” He straightened up and pointed at the headphones Sheng Wang hadn’t taken off, saying, “By the way, the report today is an exception. Starting tomorrow, mobile phones, headphones, PSPs, and things like that shouldn’t appear in the classroom. If I catch you with them, hey—” He raised his index finger and tapped it twice, then swiftly reached towards the desk of the boy in front of him. 

“Damn!” The boy immediately jumped up, quickly covering his backpack as if he had touched a live wire. “Is covering it going to help? That’s the second time, Gao Tianyang.” Director Xu raised his hand high, shaking the newly confiscated phone in front of Sheng Wang, saying, “See? This is a negative example. Where's the discipline monitor?” A girl in the front row poked her head out, “Here.” “Playing with your phone, you lose 3 points for civility. Swearing, lose 1 point.” “Oh.” Director Xu made a big show of it and left, satisfied with his spoils. Sheng Wang observed the scene closely, feeling a bit dazed. The boy named Gao Tianyang looked at him, his gaze gradually turning resentful. After a few seconds, Sheng Wang finally reacted, quietly taking off his headphones and stuffing them, along with his phone, into his backpack to avoid provoking anyone. Gao Tianyang continued to stare at him. 

After some thought, Sheng Wang politely tried to console him, saying, “Take it easy, man.” “Damn.” Gao Tianyang couldn't hold back, wiping his face in a mix of laughter and frustration, saying, “It's alright, it’s not the first time. Anyway, every once in a while they check phones, who here hasn’t been caught?” “Oh.” Sheng Wang nodded, puzzled, “So what are you looking at me for?” 

Gao Tianyang replied, “Just curious.” Sheng Wang: “?” Gao Tianyang continued, “We were just talking before you entered the classroom, and I even looked up your old school. You’re young, so why the sudden transfer to Jiangsu in your second year?” Sheng Wang chuckled awkwardly and said, “Ask my dad.” Gao Tianyang, touching his buzz cut, seemed eager to gossip more, but just then the bell rang abruptly. Classmates who had been chatting in various positions sat up straight, and several who had dozed off during the long recess sat up, stretching their arms and necks and pulling out stacks of papers from their desks. 

Once everyone returned to their seats, clustering was no longer a problem, Sheng Wang felt a strong sense of isolation—because everyone in this class was in single seats! Except him, whose desk was wedged next to another with a classmate sleeping like a corpse. Damn… Sheng Wang had just pulled out the new textbooks when he realized he couldn’t decide whether to put down his backpack or not. In sheer embarrassment, he could only turn to glare at Jiang Tian. 

His seemingly Bking classmate appeared to have pulled an all-nighter, oblivious to the ringing bell. His propped-up arm covered most of his face, leaving only his jawline visible. The white round-collar T-shirt hugged his shoulder blades, rising and falling slightly with his breath. Is he planning to sleep until dismissal? Sheng Wang thought. Gao Tianyang, sitting in front, suddenly seemed to remember something and turned around to give Jiang Tian a quick shove, whispering, “Wake up, hey Tian, self-study time.” 

He explained to Sheng Wang while pointing at Jiang Tian, “I just got him to wake up during class to avoid oversleeping.” Sheng Wang raised his eyebrows, somewhat surprised. He thought this classmate was just here to showcase his perfect sleeping record. Gao Tianyang called out twice, and Jiang Tian finally woke up. 

He mumbled a low “mm-hm,” flexed his fingers on the back of his head a few times, and the black short hair rose through his fingers. His thumb clicked against his index joint with a soft sound before he lifted his head. After sitting up straight, he rubbed his face. The struggle to wake up was visible. “What on earth did you do last night to be this tired?” Gao Tianyang couldn’t help but ask. “Just some trivial matters,” Jiang Tian clearly didn’t want to elaborate, his expression a mix of fatigue and irritation. He pulled out a bottle of mineral water from his desk; the ice fog on the bottle dispersed as he gripped it. After taking a sip, he finally caught sight of Sheng Wang. 

He frowned and turned towards him. Perhaps it was due to just drinking ice water; his tone was cool: “Who are you, and why are you sitting here?” 

What kind of question is that? Sheng Wang had already been annoyed because he was now seated next to someone with the surname Jiang, and being provoked by that tone only made him feel worse. His spoiled temperament flared up, and he pointed with his chin at the new textbook on the desk, saying, “I’m new here, so I’m sitting here. What’s it to you?” 

The exchange between handsome boys might be quite entertaining, as several classmates in the front rows turned to look. Gao Tianyang sensed the tension and quickly stepped in to smooth things over, saying, “No, you didn’t know he was assigned here just now.” “Which teacher?” Jiang Tian asked. “Who else? Big Mouth,” Gao Tianyang replied. “Doesn’t he always like to mess with seating arrangements? Last time he moved my desk next to the teacher’s podium with one sentence; then the next day, he forgot and asked why I wasn’t sitting properly in class and needed to crowd next to the teacher. I was like, what the hell?” 

Sheng Wang had been coldly facing off against Jiang Tian when he heard this and immediately turned to glare at Gao Tianyang, his face lit up with a glaring expression: Why didn’t you say anything when that Big Mouth was here? Just then, there was a sudden shuffling noise; Sheng Wang turned to see Jiang Tian standing up, dragging his chair back a bit. “What are you doing?” Gao Tianyang curiously asked. “Changing seats.” Jiang Tian said without even looking, nodding slightly in Sheng Wang’s direction, “He’s a bit shorter, so I’ll sit in the back.” 

“Who’s shorter?” Sheng Wang protested. Jiang Tian had already settled down in his new position, pulling out a thick stack of papers and tossing them on the desk. Then, leaning back against the chair, he looked up at Sheng Wang: “Otherwise, are you taller than me?” “……” 

At this point, Sheng Wang's impression of this person was completely negative and wouldn’t improve. He shifted his single desk slightly to the left to align with the row and shoved his backpack into the desk. Just as he sat down, Gao Tianyang tapped the tip of his pen on his desk, turning his head and whispering, “Hey, buddy.” “Hmm?” When Sheng Wang was in a bad mood, he would specifically not show his face to unrelated people. Gao Tianyang covered his mouth with one hand and said in an even lower voice, “Don’t take it to heart; he doesn’t usually act like this. He might be dealing with something lately and isn't in a good mood.” 

Due to politeness, Sheng Wang replied with an “oh,” but internally he thought, why should I care? Compared to that cold statue in the back, he was more concerned about the other people in the classroom. Looking around, he noticed that only his desk had textbooks on it; everyone else piled their desks with papers. Moreover, the class bell had rung for a while, yet he hadn’t seen any teachers come in. What’s wrong with this school? 

As he scanned the room, before he could even voice his confusion, caring Gao Tianyang volunteered, “It’s Saturday today, and since it’s a make-up class period, it’s all self-study for the day. You… didn’t bring any papers, did you?” 

Sheng Wang, annoyed, reminded him: “I just got here today.” “Oh, then what are you going to review with?” Gao Tianyang poked at the brand new textbook, saying, “The textbook?” “Review?” Sheng Wang echoed, “You mean review?” “Yeah.” 

Suddenly, an ominous premonition hit him, and he asked dryly, “Why do we need to review?” Gao Tianyang responded, “Because there’s a test tomorrow.” Sheng Wang froze: “???” “What’s happening tomorrow?” “A test.” 

Sheng Wang stared at him with an expression that said, you’ve got to be kidding: “What are we testing on? The first-year content?” “That was last semester’s final exam. What kind of first-year content are we testing now?” Gao Tianyang pointed at the textbook Sheng Wang had just received: “This one.” 

Sheng Wang: “……” Can you say that again? Perhaps his frozen expression looked a bit cute; Gao Tianyang laughed heartily. Sheng Wang pointed to the textbook, stating in a monotone, “Director Xu told me this is your new textbook for this semester.” “In theory, yes,” Gao Tianyang said, “But we’ve already finished it. Today’s August 8th, right? We had our summer vacation starting on July 10th; we had only ten days off before classes started, so we finished in the last couple of days.” “Which subjects?” “We finished math, physics, and chemistry; the Chinese class is slightly behind, and English doesn’t follow the textbook anyway.” 

Sheng Wang felt as if he was suffocating: “So I’m supposed to take tests on five subjects that I haven’t learned at all?” “Yes.” “Can I take a leave of absence?” “Probably not.” Gao Tianyang spoke with a mock-serious tone: “Friend, burdened with heavy responsibilities, take care of yourself. Once we graduate, just find someone to beat up Big Mouth Xu.” 

The news was so shocking that by the end of the day, Sheng Wang remained in a semi-dazed state, simply put, quite intoxicated. It wasn't until Uncle Chen, the driver, called him that he snapped back to reality, realizing self-study was over and most of the class had already left. Before leaving, Gao Tianyang seemed to have said goodbye, while the troublesome Jiang Tian had disappeared. 

On the way home, he received a call from his dad, Sheng Mingyang. A father is a father; even with a simple “mm,” he could sense something was off. “What’s wrong? Did something happen?” Sheng Wang was slumped against the car window in the backseat, lethargically saying, “There’s a request; could you please accommodate it?” “What is it?” “I want to drop out of school.” 

“……” Sheng Mingyang was taken aback, unable to suppress a laugh: “Oh, look at my son!” Sheng Wang had always been the kind of child to boast about his abilities; he only threw tantrums as a child demanding, “No!” but had never said such things as he grew up. Hearing this tone suddenly made Sheng Mingyang sentimental and softened his voice: “Come on, tell Dad, what’s going on?” 

Sighing, Sheng Wang was just about to pour out his complaints when he heard a muffled voice from beside Sheng Mingyang; it was a low voice from a woman, and Sheng Mingyang’s voice suddenly became muffled, as if he was covering the phone to respond to her. Sheng Wang paused, suddenly losing interest. 

“Nothing much, just casually mentioning it; I’ll hang up.” He forced a smile, trying to sound cheerful. “Oh, where are you?” Sheng Mingyang asked. Sheng Wang craned his neck to look out the window; the car was driving down Qingyang Street. In the distance, he could see an intersection leading to another street; a bit further up was White Horse Alley. Several food trucks were parked at the alley's entrance, steaming something; thin wisps of smoke curled against the wall by the alley entrance. 

In White Horse Alley sat his family's old ancestral home; he had moved out by the age of five. Occasionally, he would return a couple of times with his mom before he turned eight, but after she passed away, he never returned. 

The changes here were significant, and his childhood memories were not very deep. However, upon seeing that thin smoke, he unexpectedly felt a pang of nostalgia. 

When Uncle Chen drove into the courtyard, Sheng Mingyang was already waiting there. The sky was a grayish-blue, and some houses had already turned on their lights. Sheng Wang climbed out of the car, and he heard his dad softly calling out his childhood name: “Wang Zi, this is your Aunt Jiang, and this is Aunt Jiang’s son Jiang Tian, who’s a bit older than you, so call him ‘Brother.’” 

Who Jiang? Sheng Wang was taken aback, suddenly looking up.
